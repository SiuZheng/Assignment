Malaysian Cuisines

Perak

Nasi Kandar: A meal of steamed rice served with various curries and side dishes.

Sabah

Hinava: A traditional Kadazan-Dusun dish made of raw fish cured with lime juice.

Perak

Mee Rebus:This noodle dish is served in a thick, spicy-sweet gravy made from potatoes, peanuts, and shrimp paste, often garnished with hard-boiled eggs and tofu.

Melacca

Chicken Rice Balls: A unique take on the traditional chicken rice, in Melaka, the rice is shaped into small balls and served with roasted or steamed chicken.

Penang

Penang Rojak: A fruit and vegetable salad dish tossed with a thick, sweet, and spicy sauce made from shrimp paste, often topped with crushed peanuts and sesame seeds.

Langkawi

Ikan Bakar: Grilled fish marinated with spices, a favorite among seafood lovers.

Melacca

Nyonya Laksa: A rich, spicy noodle soup with a blend of Chinese and Malay flavors.

Sabah

Ambuyat: A traditional dish from the indigenous people of Sabah, Ambuyat is a starchy dish made from sago flour, often eaten with a variety of side dishes and dipping sauces.

Kuala Lumpur

Hokkien Mee: A popular dish in KL, Hokkien Mee is a stir-fried noodle dish in a dark soy sauce, typically with pork, prawns, squid, and crispy pork lard.

Penang

Char Kway Teow: Stir-fried flat rice noodles with prawns, eggs, and bean sprouts.

Kuala Lumpur

Nasi Lemak: The iconic Malaysian dish of fragrant rice cooked in coconut milk.
